B.Com In India

The Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com) is a popular undergraduate program in India that focuses on commerce, finance, accounting, taxation, and business law. Typically spanning three years and divided into six semesters, B.Com is ideal for students aiming to build careers in finance, banking, auditing, or corporate management.

Admission to B.Com programs is usually based on merit, with some universities conducting entrance exams or interviews. Top institutions offering B.Com include Delhi University, Christ University, NMIMS, Loyola College, and St. Xavier’s College. Students can choose between general B.Com or specialized tracks like B.Com (Hons), B.Com in Accounting & Finance, or B.Com in Banking & Insurance.

B.Com graduates often pursue careers in accounting firms, financial services, government sectors, or continue with higher studies like M.Com, MBA, CA, or CMA. The degree provides a strong foundation for roles in commerce, taxation, and financial analysis, both in India and abroad.

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must have completed 10+2 from a recognized board, preferably with Commerce or related subjects.

Minimum 45%–50% aggregate marks are required (relaxation for reserved categories).

Admission is usually merit-based, though some universities may conduct entrance exams or interviews.

Students from any stream—Commerce, Science, or Arts—can apply, depending on the college’s criteria.
