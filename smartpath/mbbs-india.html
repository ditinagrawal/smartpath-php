MBBS In India

MBBS Admission in India

MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery) is one of the most prestigious and competitive undergraduate programs in India. The course spans 5.5 years, including one year of mandatory internship in clinical settings. Admission to MBBS programs is strictly based on the NEET-UG entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), which is mandatory for all government and private medical colleges.

Every year, lakhs of students appear for NEET with the dream of becoming a doctor. India has over 600 medical colleges offering more than 1 lakh MBBS seats through various quotas like All India Quota (AIQ), State Quota, Management Quota, and NRI Quota. After qualifying NEET, students must participate in centralized counselling conducted by MCC or respective state authorities. MBBS graduates go on to serve in hospitals, clinics, research institutions, and pursue higher studies like MD/MS. With its strong academic foundation and clinical exposure, MBBS remains the top choice for students passionate about healthcare and medicine.

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must have passed the 10+2 examination with Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Biotechnology, and English as core subjects.

A minimum of 50% marks in PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) is required for General category students, and 40% for reserved categories.

Admission to MBBS programs is strictly based on the NEET-UG entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA).

Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission or by December 31 of the admission year.

Admission Process for MBBS in India

MBBS admissions in India: We help answer the two most important questions — How does one become a medical student in India? How can I enroll in an MBBS program in India?

Admission to MBBS programs is strictly based on the NEET-UG entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA).

After qualifying NEET, candidates must participate in centralized counselling conducted by MCC (for All India Quota) or respective state authorities.

Seats are allotted based on NEET rank, category, and preferences filled during counselling. Quotas include AIQ, State, Management, and NRI.

How to Choose a Medical College

Before selecting a medical college, it’s essential to understand your long-term goals in the field of medicine. Whether you aim to become a general practitioner, surgeon, or pursue research, choosing the right institution can shape your future. It’s also wise to keep backup options based on NEET rank, location preference, and fee structure.
