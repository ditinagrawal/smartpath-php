MBBS IN ABROAD

MBBS Admission Abroad

MBBS abroad has become a popular choice for Indian students seeking quality medical education at affordable costs. Countries like Russia, Bangladesh, Georgia, and the Caribbean offer globally recognized MBBS programs with modern infrastructure and experienced faculty. These programs typically span five to six years, including clinical rotations and practical training.

With limited seats and intense competition in Indian medical colleges, thousands of students opt for international universities approved by the National Medical Commission (NMC). Admission is generally based on NEET qualification, and many universities offer simplified application processes without entrance exams. Graduates from these institutions often pursue licensing exams like FMGE or USMLE to practice medicine in India or abroad.

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must successfully complete the 10+2 examination with at least 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB).

Qualifying the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) is mandatory for Indian students seeking MBBS admission abroad.

Some universities may require English proficiency tests like IELTS or TOEFL, depending on the country and medium of instruction.

Students must ensure the foreign medical university is recognized by the National Medical Commission (NMC) to be eligible for FMGE licensing in India.

Admission Process For MBBS Abroad

MBBS admissions abroad: We help answer the two most important questions—How can Indian students pursue MBBS overseas? What steps are involved in applying to top international medical universities?

Students must qualify the NEET exam, which is mandatory for pursuing MBBS abroad and for future licensing in India.

Research and shortlist NMC-approved universities in countries like Russia, Bangladesh, Georgia, and the Caribbean.

Submit your application along with academic documents, passport copy, and NEET scorecard to the selected university.

Upon receiving the admission letter, apply for a student visa, pay tuition fees, and plan travel and accommodation.

How to Choose a College Abroad

Before selecting a medical university abroad, it’s essential to understand your long-term goals—whether you plan to practice in India or internationally. Choose a country and college that aligns with your budget, language preference, and licensing pathway. Always keep a few backup options in case your preferred university has limited seats or stricter eligibility.

MBBS programs abroad are available in countries like Russia, Georgia, Bangladesh, and the Philippines, offering globally recognized degrees and modern infrastructure. Many universities follow English-medium instruction and are approved by the National Medical Commission (NMC), making them suitable for Indian students.
