Allied Health Science In India

Allied Health Science is a vital segment of healthcare in India, offering specialized training in diagnostic, therapeutic, and technical services. Popular undergraduate programs include B.Sc in Medical Laboratory Technology, Operation Theatre Technology, and Radiology.

Admission is typically merit-based or through entrance exams conducted by universities or state bodies. Leading institutions include AIIMS, Manipal Academy of Higher Education, JIPMER, and SRM University. Students gain practical skills in lab procedures, imaging, and clinical support.

Graduates can pursue careers in hospitals, diagnostic centers, rehabilitation clinics, or continue with advanced studies like M.Sc Allied Health or hospital administration. The field is growing rapidly with strong demand across India and abroad.

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must have completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology or Mathematics, depending on the specialization.

Minimum 45%–50% aggregate marks are required (relaxation for reserved categories as per norms).

Admission is generally merit-based, though some universities conduct entrance exams or interviews.

Age limit typically starts from 17 years; upper age limits may vary by institution.

Admission Process For Allied Health Science In India

Allied Health Science admissions in India help students begin their journey in diagnostic and technical healthcare roles. Here’s how to apply for top allied health colleges:

Admission to B.Sc Allied Health programs is usually merit-based, based on Class 12 marks.

Some universities conduct entrance exams or interviews for specific specializations.

Applicants may also undergo medical fitness checks or counseling rounds during final selection.

Choosing the right Allied Health Science college begins with identifying your interest—whether in lab technology, radiology, physiotherapy, or surgical support. It’s important to assess colleges based on hands-on training, lab infrastructure, faculty expertise, and clinical exposure.
