LLB In India

The Bachelor of Legislative Law (LLB) is a foundational undergraduate program in legal education, designed for students aspiring to become lawyers, legal advisors, or pursue careers in judiciary, corporate law, or public service. LLB is offered in two formats: a 3-year program for graduates and a 5-year integrated course (e.g., BA LLB, BBA LLB) for students after Class 12.

Admission to LLB programs is based on entrance exams such as CLAT, AILET, LSAT India, CUET LLB, or university-specific tests. Top law colleges include National Law Universities (NLUs), Faculty of Law – Delhi University, Symbiosis Law School, Jindal Global Law School, and Government Law College, Mumbai.

LLB students study subjects like constitutional law, criminal law, contract law, jurisprudence, and legal procedures. Graduates can pursue careers in litigation, corporate law, civil services, or continue with higher studies like LLM or judicial preparation. The degree builds strong analytical, research, and advocacy skills essential for legal practice.

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must have completed 10+2 from a recognized board for 5-year integrated LLB programs, or hold a bachelor’s degree for 3-year LLB courses.

Minimum 45%–50% aggregate marks are required (relaxation for reserved categories as per norms).

Admission is based on entrance exams like CLAT, AILET, LSAT India, CUET LLB, or university-specific tests.

Students from any stream—Arts, Commerce, or Science—can apply for integrated law programs after Class 12.

Choosing the right LLB college begins with understanding your legal career goals—whether you aim to practice litigation, join corporate law, pursue civil services, or specialize in areas like criminal or constitutional law. It’s important to evaluate colleges based on faculty experience, moot court exposure, curriculum strength, and placement support.
